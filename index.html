📖How to use each section
▼
📋Content Planner›

Your visual board for tracking every piece of content from first idea to published. Move cards through the stages as you work.

  • Idea stage: dump anything here — half-formed thoughts count. Just get it out of your head.
  • In Progress: move it here when you've started writing, designing, or recording.
  • Ready to Post: caption written, graphic done, hashtags ready — waiting to go live.
  • Published: move it here once it's live so you can see your output at a glance.
  • Tip: add format (Carousel, Newsletter, Freebie, Event) so you can see content variety at a glance.
✅Tasks›

A simple three-column board for your to-do list. Separate from the Content Planner — use this for business tasks, admin, client work, anything you need to act on.

  • To Do: everything you need to do. Add freely — you can prioritize later.
  • In Progress: what you're actively working on right now. Try to keep this list short.
  • Done: move it here when complete. Seeing things in Done feels good — let it motivate you.
  • Tip: start each week by reviewing To Do and picking 3 things that will actually move the needle.
📅Calendar›

Your monthly publishing map. Click any day to add a content entry — you'll see colored dots on the calendar showing what's planned.

  • Use it for: mapping out when specific content goes live, not for writing the content itself.
  • Statuses: Idea → Draft → Scheduled → Published. Update as you move through your workflow.
  • Tip: aim to have next week planned by Friday. Even placeholder entries help you stay on track.
  • Color dots: each platform gets its own color so you can see balance at a glance — are you posting on all your platforms?
✦AI Studio›

Your AI writing partner, powered by Claude. Give it a topic and it generates a full draft in your voice — no more staring at a blank page.

  • Business Context matters most: the more you fill in Settings → Business Context, the more on-brand every draft will be.
  • Give it a specific brief: instead of "write a caption about my business," try "write a caption about why wellness entrepreneurs burn out when they don't delegate — warm tone, end with a DM CTA."
  • Save your drafts: hit Save Draft to add it to your library. You can always come back and copy or reference it later.
  • Use Hooks first: grab a hook from the Hooks library, drop it into the brief, and ask Claude to build from it.
🔗Hooks Library›

Pre-written opening lines designed to stop the scroll. Filter by type and copy directly into your content or into the AI Studio as a starting point.

  • Educational hooks: best for carousels, blog posts, and reels that teach something.
  • Engagement hooks: great for posts where you want comments, shares, or saves.
  • Storytelling hooks: use these when you're sharing a personal experience or client story.
  • Add your own: when you write a hook that performs well, save it here so you can reuse and riff on it.
💬Prompts, CTAs & Story Ideas›

Three libraries of ready-to-copy content pieces. Prompts are for Claude, CTAs go at the end of your content, Story Ideas are for Instagram Stories.

  • Prompts: copy a prompt, paste it into AI Studio or Claude directly, fill in the brackets with your specifics.
  • CTAs: always end your content with a clear call to action — pick one that matches your goal for that post (engagement, sales, or growth).
  • Story Ideas: use these when you open Instagram and have no idea what to post — they're designed to be quick and authentic.
  • Favorite: hit the heart on anything you love so you can find it again fast.
🧠Brain Dump›

Unlimited notebooks for anything that doesn't fit neatly anywhere else. Thoughts, ideas, client notes, inspiration, lists — no wrong way to use it.

  • Suggested notebooks: Content Ideas Parking Lot, Client Notes, Weekly Reflection, Inspiration & Research, Business Planning.
  • Brain dump freely: when your brain is busy, just open a note and type. Don't edit, don't organize — just get it out. You can sort it later.
  • Content Parking Lot: ideas that aren't ready for the Content Planner yet. Keep them here until you have bandwidth.
  • Tip: review your Brain Dump notebooks weekly during your planning session — that's when you move things to the Planner.

Welcome to your Studio ✦
This is your personal content workspace. Here's how it all works — read this once and you'll feel right at home.
1

Set up your settings first

Go to Settings and add your studio name and Brand Profile. The Brand Profile is the most important part — it tells Claude who you are, who you serve, and what you offer so every draft sounds like you.

2

Use the Library to spark ideas

Hooks, Prompts, CTAs, and Story Ideas are all pre-loaded for your wellness/VA niche. Browse, filter by category, and copy anything that resonates. Add your own anytime.

3

Plan content in the Content Planner

Add posts, carousels, newsletters, freebies, events — anything you're creating. Move each one from Idea → In Progress → Ready → Published as you go. Nothing falls through the cracks.

4

Plan it on the Calendar

Add calendar entries to map out when content will go live. You can see everything by month and track what's scheduled vs. published.

5

Generate drafts in AI Studio

Pick your content type, platform, and tone — give Claude a brief, and get a full draft. Save it to your library, copy it, or use it as a starting point.

6

Your data is saved automatically

Everything you create saves to your account instantly and syncs across all your devices. Pick up on your phone exactly where you left off on your laptop.
